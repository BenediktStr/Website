# First Blog Post

Welcome to my creative journey. This is where I tinker with ideas, work through challenges, and write about the things that captivate me—from personal to professional. I want to start with my professional life, because there have been and continue to be challenges along the way.

I am a **Fullstack Developer**, but I have quickly discovered my biggest challenge: *too many interests* within software engineering alone. Frontend, Backend, Crypto, or DevOps—you name it, I am drawn to all of them.

## My Framework

Over the past few months, I came to a realization that I have to be intentional about where I invest my professional time and energy. So I designed a simple framework for myself, which I will follow for the foreseeable future. It is not perfect, but it is my attempt to leap in the right direction.

1. Deepen my job skills as a developer in my day job
2. Master the fundamentals upon which much of my profession is built
3. Move in a career direction which is promising and also interests me

### Okay, that sounds logical, but what are my actual steps?

1. **Current Role**: As a Fullstack Developer with a 70/30 split, my focus here is to improve my skills in proportion to daily use.
2. **Building Foundations**: Figuring out the fundamentals took time, but in the end I landed on Linux. I already have basic CLI knowledge from past DevOps work, but I decided to truly master it. So I switched my personal PC from Windows to Arch Linux. And oh boy, it is way harder than I thought. But in the end, a strong Linux foundation is more or less necessary for the next part.
3. **Direction**: DevOps. While I might refine the details, the direction is clear. I already have solid DevOps experience, and it's a universally applicable skill. Early in my career, I felt lost navigating the gap between writing code and shipping it. DevOps bridges that gap. Kubernetes, Ansible and Cloud infrastructure—these tools finally made that journey tangible.

## Current Progress

I'm already in motion. Next week, I'm finishing my first Azure certification. In parallel, I'm building out my Kubernetes homelab, sharpening my Fullstack skills, and improving my writing. Running my own infrastructure on Kubernetes has shifted from a distant goal to an active project.
